Privacy Policy

Last updated 10 July 2026  ·  Krixo Labs

This page describes exactly what leaves your phone, who processes it, and how to get rid of it. We have tried to write it in plain language rather than boilerplate, and to be precise where precision matters.

The short version

  • Your book files never leave your device. The PDF or EPUB itself is stored only on your phone. We never upload it, and we never store a copy.
  • The text inside your books does leave, once. To make a book searchable and to answer questions about it, its text is sent to our servers and on to our AI providers. We do not store that text on our servers; the resulting search index is stored on your phone.
  • Your highlights, notes and progress are stored in our database so they sync across devices and survive a reinstall.
  • We do not sell your data, and we do not show you advertising.
  • You can delete everything from inside the app, and it is really deleted.

Who we are

Krixo is operated by Krixo Labs, India. For anything in this policy, including privacy requests, write to support@krixo.in.

What we collect

Account information

Your email address and display name. If you sign up with a password, we store a bcrypt hash of it, never the password itself. If you sign in with Google, we receive your email address, name and Google account identifier, and we store the first two.

Your reading data

Highlights, notes, drawings, bookmarks, reading progress, saved vocabulary, flashcards and their review history, and your conversations with the AI about your books. Also book metadata: title, author, page count, language and a content hash used to recognise the same book across devices.

Book content

This is the part people care about most, so it deserves detail rather than a slogan.

  • The book file stays on your device. There is no code path that uploads it.
  • When you add a book, its text is split into passages and sent to our servers, which forward them to Voyage AI to convert into numeric vectors. Those vectors come back and are stored on your phone. We do not keep the text or the vectors.
  • When you use Ask, Explain, Notes, quizzes or Audio Overview, the relevant text is sent to our AI providers to produce the answer. Audio Overview summarises the whole book, so it sends the whole book's text.
  • If you screenshot part of a page and ask about it, that image is stored in our storage and sent to a vision model.
  • If a book is a scanned PDF, its page images are sent to Google Cloud Vision to extract the text.
  • Searching your library happens on your phone, against the index stored there. Only your question and the few matching passages are sent for an answer.

Technical information

Your IP address, used to guess your country so we can show the right currency, and to apply rate limits. A device identifier, so the app knows which of your devices holds which book. If you enable reminders, a push token and your time zone.

Payments

Payments are processed by Razorpay. Your card and banking details go to Razorpay, never to us. We store only your subscription status, when it started, when it expires, and the payment identifier.

Everything else in Krixo works without them.

We do not use your content to train our own models, and we do not sell or rent your data to anyone. Our providers process your content only to return a result to us. Each has its own terms and retention practices, which we do not control.

How long we keep it

  • Delete a book and its highlights, notes, vocabulary, flashcards, chats, screenshots and cached text are removed from our servers and from your device.
  • Delete your account and everything above is removed, along with your account record. This is not a soft delete.
  • Text sent to AI providers is not stored by us. Provider-side retention is governed by their policies.
  • Records we must keep for tax or accounting, such as payment receipts, are retained as long as the law requires.

Your rights

You can see, correct, export and delete your data. Most of it is in the app already: Settings to manage your account, and Notes to export your highlights and notes as PDF, Markdown, CSV or an Anki deck. To delete your account, use Settings → Delete account. If you would rather we did it, email support@krixo.in and we will action it within 30 days.

Depending on where you live, you may also have the right to object to processing, to restrict it, or to complain to a data protection authority. Indian users may contact our grievance officer at the same address.

Security

Traffic to our servers is encrypted with TLS. Passwords are hashed with bcrypt. Our application server has no public network ingress and sits behind a load balancer and a web application firewall. Password reset links are single use and expire after an hour, and resetting a password signs out every device that was already signed in.

No system is perfectly secure. If you find a vulnerability, please tell us at support@krixo.in before disclosing it publicly, and we will work with you.

Children

Krixo is not directed at children under 13, and we do not knowingly collect their data. If you believe a child has given us information, write to us and we will delete it.

Changes

If we change this policy in a way that materially affects you, we will tell you in the app before it takes effect. The date at the top always reflects the current version.
